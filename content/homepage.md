Tone of Voice Guide
•	Confident, not boastful — we state what we do clearly without superlatives.
•	Human and direct — we write for the founder or CFO reading this at 9 pm, not for a committee.
•	Technically precise but plain-English — jargon is used only where necessary; always explained.
•	Trustworthy — we do not promise outcomes, we describe capability and process.
•	Commercially aware — we understand that clients care about business outcomes, not just compliance.
•	Premium but approachable — warm enough that a first-generation entrepreneur feels welcome, serious enough that a multinational CFO feels confident.
•	Indian context first — we reference Indian law, Indian regulatory bodies, and Indian business realities without apology.

CTA Strategy
The website should use two types of CTA throughout:
•	Primary CTA: 'Speak to a Partner' or 'Schedule a Consultation' — direct, no-friction, appropriate for a professional services context.
•	Secondary CTA: 'Explore our work in [sector/service]' — content-led, for visitors who are still in research mode.
Every service page should end with a pain-point-to-CTA bridge: 'If this is a challenge you are facing, we would be glad to talk it through.'
